Description Lists

solid
Matter with a fixed shape and volume. The particles are closely packed and vibrate in place, making solids rigid. Examples include ice, wood, and metal.

liquid
Matter that has a fixed volume but takes the shape of its container. The particles are less tightly packed than in solids, allowing them to flow. Examples include water, oil, and milk.

gas
Matter that has neither a fixed shape nor a fixed volume. Gas particles are spread out and move freely, filling whatever space is available. Examples include oxygen, nitrogen, and carbon dioxide.

plasma
A high-energy state where gas particles become ionized (charged) due to extreme heat or electromagnetic forces. Examples include lightning and the Sun's surface.
